About

Behavioural scientist. Applied researcher. Mostly asking why people do what they do.

I came to UX research through a question about why people look at what they look at. I started my PhD at UBC's Brain, Attention & Reality Lab in 2019 studying social attention — how our awareness of what our gaze signals to others changes where we actually look. That question turned out to be a product question in disguise: the same mechanism shapes how users navigate interfaces where their activity is visible, collaborative, or judged.

Over six years the question expanded into the cognitive infrastructure behind every decision users make in digital environments — where they direct attention, what makes them trust information, when they feel confident, and how visual complexity changes what they can accomplish. I ran 10+ studies with 1,600+ participants and published in Cognition, Attention, Perception, & Psychophysics, and Cognitive Science.

Halfway through, I took a research role at CheckingIn, a B2B health-tech startup — the only researcher, embedded with leadership. The moment I'm proudest of isn't a paper; it's when I adapted a protocol mid-study because participants kept raising a question I hadn't asked, and that unscripted thread became the finding that changed what the product was. I'm now looking for a research role where both the rigour and the pragmatism are valued.

Outside research I'm usually somewhere between a soccer pitch, a book I'm trying to finish, and figuring out why my R code is running slower than it should.
